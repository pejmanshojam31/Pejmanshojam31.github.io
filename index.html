About Me

I recently submitted my PhD in Mathematical Biology at TU Dresden, where I was affiliated with the Center for Interdisciplinary Digital Sciences (CIDS) and the Department of High-Performance Computing (ZIH). My research focused on predictive modeling of glioblastoma recurrence by integrating mathematical models, radiomics, and machine learning. I developed hybrid in silico frameworks to simulate tumor-macrophage interactions and support personalized treatment strategies. With a background in biomedical and mechanical engineering, I bring interdisciplinary expertise in computational oncology, image analysis, and AI-driven diagnostics. I am currently working at HTW Dresden on radiomics-based prediction of tumor spheroid dynamics.
